About hemophilia

In hemophilia, the human body cannot naturally stop bleeding, which occurs when a blood vessel is damaged. Such bleeding can occur from trauma as well as from stress. If the bleeding is not stopped and treated, it can lead to joint damage (e.g., to the ankle, knee, or elbow) as well as further disabilities and limitations in movement (in the worst case, the person has the risk of losing a limb).
